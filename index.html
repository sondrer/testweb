Research

Secure Communication

Advanced quantum-resistant encryption protocols for secure space communications

Our secure communication research focuses on developing quantum-resistant cryptographic protocols specifically designed for space environments. This includes lattice-based cryptography, code-based schemes, and implementation security for resource-constrained satellite hardware.

Satellite Networks

Autonomous satellite constellation management and secure networking protocols

Satellite network research encompasses autonomous constellation management, secure inter-satellite communication protocols, and robust networking algorithms for deep space missions. We develop AI-driven solutions for dynamic network topology management.

Defense Systems

Next-generation space-based defense and monitoring systems

Our defense systems research includes advanced space-based monitoring capabilities, threat detection algorithms, and secure command and control systems for national security applications in space.

Projects

Current research projects

High Assurance Satellite Communication

Design of module for high assurance space communication: maximizing security and speed with minimal footprint

This project focuses on implementing quantum-resistant encryption algorithms in satellite communication systems. Project funded by Ministry of Defense through NSM to develop high speed quantum resilient cryptographic module for resource constrained satellite hardware. Keywords: Implementation security, formal verification. The goal is to maximize security and speed, while minimizing resource usage. PL: Sondre Rønjom

Hardware Security Modules for Space

Secure hardware implementations for satellite onboard systems

Research into tamper-resistant hardware security modules specifically designed for space environments. Includes formal verification methods and radiation-hardened cryptographic implementations under the direction of Henrik Fegran.

Formal Verification of Space Systems

Mathematical verification of satellite communication protocols

Application of formal methods and mathematical verification techniques to ensure correctness of satellite communication protocols. Led by Håkon Gylterud with contributions from Gabriel Slettvoll on automated theorem proving.

Satellite Constellation Security

Secure distributed satellite systemss

Development of secure communication protocols for satellite constellations, including fault-tolerant consensus algorithms and distributed key management systems. Research focuses on autonomous satellite networks and secure inter-satellite links, and distributed quantum sensing for space awarenes
